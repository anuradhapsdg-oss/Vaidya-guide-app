What is Ayurveda?

Ayurveda is an ancient system of natural healing that originated in India over 5,000 years ago.

Its core principle is that every individual possesses a unique mind-body constitution comprised of three vital forces, or Doshas: Vata, Pitta, and Kapha.

"Balancing these sacred energies is the gateway to vibrant health, mental clarity, and longevity."

Vata Dosha

The Energy of Movement. Elements of Air & Ether. Governs respiration, circulation, and nervous impulses.

Pitta Dosha

The Energy of Transformation. Elements of Fire & Water. Governs digestion, metabolic rates, and cognitive sharp intellect.

Kapha Dosha

The Energy of Structure. Elements of Earth & Water. Governs somatic coherence, joints lubrication, and structural stability.

Pure Sustenance

Vegetarian Nutrition by Dosha

In Ayurveda, pure food is considered your absolute primary medicine. Select nutritional guidance customized for your constitution.

Vata Diet

Vata Balance

Recommended: warm soups, thoroughly cooked grains, roasted root vegetables, wholesome nuts, ripe avocado, sweet dates, coconut, and ghee.
Avoid: iced cold drinks, excessively dry crackers, raw cabbage, tart apples, and cold raw salads.
Pitta Diet

Pitta Balance

Recommended: cooling cucumbers, summer zucchini, fresh broccoli, sweet coconut flesh, sweet organic melons, premium ghee, and split mung dal.
Avoid: sharp spicy chilies, heavily fried food, acidic tomatoes, garlic, and vinegar-rich items.
Kapha Diet

Kapha Balance

Recommended: dense leafy greens, dry organic lentils, freshly grated ginger root, buckwheat, baked organic apples, fresh pomegranate, and raw honey.
Avoid: heavy dairy, frozen desserts, processed white flour, and excessively salty fried snacks.
